Using Recruitment Automation to Improve Hiring

Hiring is a resource intensive process, but it doesn't have to be. Paycor explains how recruitment automation can save your team time and money.

Talent Management
Last Updated:  March 25, 2020 | Read Time: 6 min
In today’s fast-paced work environment, automation is making its way into all aspects of business. The recruitment industry is no exception to this automation takeover with automated job-board postings, resume-screeners and automated interviews being just a few tools in the recruitment automation landscape.
Recruitment automation is the process of augmenting and enhancing recruiters’ abilities through the use of smart tools designed to expedite and improve the hiring process. Automation is everywhere nowadays, and in recent years it has become more and more intertwined with everyday recruiting processes. Using an automated recruiting system and employing the best practices that go along with it can be a huge boost to any company’s hiring process.
As a recruiter or hiring manager, no matter how hard you work, there is always more that could be done and areas that could be improved. Unfortunately, with shrinking resources and increasing expectations, recruiters are being asked to do more with less than ever before. This is where automation comes in. By automating some of the more redundant and time-consuming tasks, you are able to make a significant improvement in what you are able to accomplish on a daily basis.
The quality of your hires needs to stay high, and recruitment automation helps take some of the more mundane tasks out of your hands so you can focus on what really matters. Recruitment automation can also help by providing data on previous and current hires so you can replicate success in your recruitment efforts to find quality candidates.
There are many benefits to using recruitment automation in your hiring process. Whether you are just starting to build out your team or you’re hiring thousands of people per year, automation tools such as resume screeners and personalized emails will help to streamline your recruitment efforts. However, with this in mind it is crucial to never lose the human element. At the end of the day, a business is made up of people wanting to employ more people. Fully automating everything about your hiring process can end up hurting more than it helps as people still want to feel as if they are interacting with humans, especially when it comes to a major life decision like accepting a new job offer.
